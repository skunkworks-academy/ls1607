## Development gaps

| Learning sequence | Limit active focus to one primary pathway and one secondary project. |
| Practical evidence | Convert activity into repositories, diagrams, reports and demonstrations. |

| Career transition | Align Year 12, ADF, tertiary study and employment decisions. |

## Development principles

1. **Foundation before specialisation.** Networking, Linux and operating-system fluency precede advanced security tooling.
2. **Evidence before claims.** Every skill should result in a repository, lab record, diagram, incident note or demonstration.
3. **Security by design.** The OpenClaw agent is treated as a system requiring boundaries, controls and auditability.
4. **One primary focus.** Cybersecurity is the main path; secure AI automation is the supporting project context.
5. **Review-driven progression.** Advancement depends on evidence reviewed at defined gates, not time elapsed alone.

## Development constraints

- Year 12 remains the first academic priority.
- The ADF gap-year programme will change available time and operating context.
- Luca currently lacks a local cybersecurity peer group.
- Early technical enthusiasm may cause over-expansion across too many platforms.
- Local-agent experimentation can introduce privacy, credential and remote-access risk without deliberate controls.
